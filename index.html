Powering the future through hydrogen!

Hydrogen fuel cells combine hydrogen and oxygen to produce electricity, heat, and water.

What is it?

A hydrogen fuel cell is a device that converts chemical potential energy into electrical energy.

Hydrogen fuel cells make use of electrochemical reactions of hydrogen and oxygen gas to produce electricity.


As the only byproduct is water and heat, hydrogen fuel cells are a big improvement over traditional electrical generation methods such as coal and nuclear power plants.


Hydrogen fuel cells are used in many different applications such as automobiles, backup power generation, and spacecraft.

The Process

How do we go from hydrogen gas to producing electricity?
01

01. Anode

H2 from a tank is fed through a pipe and enters the anode.

02

02. Cathode

O2 from the air comes down another pipe and enters the cathode.

03

03. Catalyst

The catalyst splits up the hydrogen gas into H+ and e-. Electrons travel through an outer circuit, producing a current.

04

04. Electrolyte

The electrolyte blocks electrons and allow only H+ to pass through. H+ and e- combine with O2 to produce H2O

Societal Benefits

Hydrogen fuel cells provide a greater advantage in electrical generation over many conventional methods.
Costs are lower

Hydrogen fuel cells cost little to maintain and over the past few years, the costs have been lowered significantly. Moreover, because it runs on hydrogen, it cuts out the need for importing of fossil fuels and natural gas. This is especially beneficial for developing nations.

Clean and pollution-free

The only byproduct is water and heat. No harmful emissions are produced throughout the electrochemical reaction. Everyone will be able to enjoy a cleaner environment and will leave a smaller footprint.

Readily Available

The abundance of hydrogen on our planet makes fueling the cell very easy and convenient. Hydrogen gas can be produced wherever water is available, cutting the need for importing. Since water is a renewable resource, hydrogen fuel cells can produce electricity indefinitely.

Reliable and Efficient

Because of the high reliability and efficiency, hydrogen fuel cells are favoured as they can help prevent power outages, reducing the risks for businesses or serve as backup power. They are also much more efficient compared to traditional combustion technology.

Environmental and Health & Safety Concerns

  • 01

    Hydrogen gas has a lower ignition energy than other chemicals like gasoline meaning it can ignite more easily. Hydrogen also burns almost invisible so the flame cannot be seen.

  • 02

    Some materials can become brittle when exposed to hydrogen gas. Appropriate materials must be selected when designing a hydrogen system.

  • 03

    If hydrogen gas leaks from the tank, it has the chance to asphyxiate people, depriving them of oxygen which could cause death. As hydrogen gas is invisible, you may not know if you are breathing in hydrogen gas.

  • 04

    To produce hydrogen gas, natural gas is used in the process, which will release CO2 and pollute the environment

  • 05

    If hydrogen gas leaks during the production or use, it could eat up the ozone layer and create a larger problem.

Motivation For His Research

Grove’s motivation seemed to root from his interest in science.

Though he studied law and classics, it is speculated that Baden Powell, a mathematician, influenced Grove’s love for science.

After his marriage with Emma Maria Powells, Grove toured the continent for their honeymoon. The sabbatical gave Grove an opportunity to pursue his interest in science.

During his honeymoon, Grove wrote his first scientific paper which consisted of new constructions for electric cells.

Other Accomplishment(s) For Which He Is Known

William Robert Grove was the founder of Swansea Literary and Philosophical Society in 1835.

He published “On the Correlation of Physical Forces” in 1842 as a proposal for the conservation of energy.

In 1871, Grove was appointed to the Court of Common Pleas and was knighted the following year.

Hydrogen Fuel Cell Vehicles

The hydrogen fuel cell produces electricity on board to power the electric motors of vehicles.

Fast refuelling compared to regular electric vehicles: 10 minutes to achieve 200 - 300 mile range.

Cuts emissions down by 30% compared to traditional combustion engine vehicles.

Regenerative Braking converts the energy wasted during braking back into electricity.

How Does This Relate to Electrochemistry?

A hydrogen fuel cell uses an electrochemical reaction to produce a spontaneous redox reaction.
